02 — Challenge

A Blank
Slate.

When this project landed, the product had no navigation structure defined — none. No existing model to refine, no legacy patterns to honour.

We were designing inside a large-scale enterprise banking platform where multiple user types — business operators, compliance teams, and system admins — all had to accomplish fundamentally different jobs inside the same product.

The challenge wasn't to redesign something broken. It was to design something from nothing, and get it right the first time.

03 — Navigation Strategy

Group by Object. Stay in Context.

01

Business Object Grouping

Navigation is built around what the user is managing — accounts, loans, compliance records, transactions — not the system's internal modules. Users think in terms of their job, not the software.

Information Architecture

02

Workspace Model

Each business object has its own dedicated workspace — a persistent context where the user completes the full lifecycle of a task. Everything relevant stays in one place, no jumping between screens.

UX Architecture

03

Main Bank Context

The top-level "main bank" is the user's persistent anchor point. Navigate into any workspace and always know where you are and how to get back. No deep drilling, no getting lost in the product.

Wayfinding

04 — Design Pattern

Think
Slow.
Act Fast.

Enterprise users operate in two distinct modes. When configuring rules and setting up workflows, they need to think carefully — errors cascade. When executing daily operations, they need to act fast with zero friction.

The interface was designed to structurally separate these two modes, so the speed of interaction matched the stakes of the task.

06 — Outcome

Stakeholder

The navigation architecture was presented to product leadership, engineering leads, and key banking stakeholders. Sign-off secured without major revisions.
